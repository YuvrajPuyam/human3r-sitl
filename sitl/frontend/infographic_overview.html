BODYPLOT — ANALYSIS CAPABILITIES

6 behavioral measurements derived from 3D SMPL-X tracking · grounded in published social science frameworks
enriched_data.json · 3–5 MB per video
Proxemics Zones
Per-frame pairwise distance between all subjects, classified into Hall's four interpersonal space zones. Edges rendered in the 3D viewer: orange for personal, red for contact range.
intimate < 0.45 m · personal 0.45–1.2 m
social 1.2–3.7 m · public > 3.7 m
Hall, E.T. (1966). The Hidden Dimension.
Gaze Direction
Body-facing vector computed per person per frame as the cross product of the normalized shoulder vector and spine vector — more stable than head orientation. Gaze convergence events logged when any pair's rays approach within 1 m.
gaze = normalize(cross(body_up, shoulder))
convergence: closest_approach < 1.0 m
Kendon (1967); Argyle & Cook (1976). Mutual gaze in social interaction.
Action Classification
Six action classes predicted per person per frame using MotionBERT temporal features and biomechanical rules on SMPL-X rotation vectors. Knee flexion angle from pose vectors distinguishes sitting from standing with high stability.
■ stationary   ■ walking   ■ running
■ sitting   ■ reaching   ■ bending
Wu et al. (2022) MotionBERT · 15-frame majority-vote smoothing.
Surface Contact
Contact score per person per frame computed via KD-tree nearest-neighbour query from the estimated foot position against the scene point cloud. Scores near 1.0 indicate the person is standing on or near a surface.
foot = pelvis + [0, +0.95, 0] (Y-DOWN)
score = exp(−d²) where d = dist to PLY
Requires fused scene.ply from Human3R reconstruction stage.
Trajectories & Approach
Consistent person IDs maintained across frames for trajectory extraction. Approach events counted on state transitions: when a pair's Δdistance crosses −0.03 m/frame from non-negative. Retreat symmetrically tracked.
approach: prev ≠ approaching AND Δd < −0.03 m
speed filter: skip jumps > 0.35 m/frame
Goffman (1971) Interaction ritual; approach–avoidance dynamics.
Floor Heatmap
Bird's-eye occupancy density map generated from all pelvis XZ positions across the video. Gaussian-smoothed and rendered as a PNG texture on a floor plane in the 3D viewer. Shows spatial patterns without a top-down camera.
grid: cell_size = 0.12 m · σ = nx × 0.025
bounds from world-coordinate XZ extent + 0.7 m margin
Scene utilization: occupied 0.5 m voxels / total scene voxels × 100%.
